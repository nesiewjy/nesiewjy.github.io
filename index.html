Nesie Junyi Wang (b. 1998, China) works with the uncertainty and complexity of human interactions with their immediate environments. With a background in photography and printmaking, her practice engages with social and ecological discourses, tracing the tensions, adaptations, and transformations that emerge among beings in conditioned landscapes.

She earned a BA in Photography from the Royal Academy of Art, The Hague (KABK) in 2024. Currently, she is a resident artist at Delft University of Technology, where she investigates the use of extracellular polymeric substances (EPS) recovered from wastewater treatment processes as pigment materials.
